Is it bad to not be smart and intelligent as the rest of common people?

Of course it’s OK not to be (what other people call) ‘smart’. For one thing, smart people aren’t always smart. I can tell you stories of ‘geniuses’ who are so involved in their pet projects they forget to eat. Some, who are particularly valuable, have minders to clean them up for special occasions. That’s not always a pleasant job because the minder probably has to bathe a person who hasn’t taken care of their personal hygiene for some time. There are stories of them finding sandwiches and other food items in pockets. So, if you can take care of your personal hygiene, manage your money, get to work on time, and generally complete your daily tasks satisfactorily, you may actually be more valuable than a ‘genius’ who doesn’t. Besides, ‘smart’ is a very narrow area. There are many perfectly average people who contribute a great deal to society. They can be loving, caring, emotionally stable, spiritual, artistic, the list of attributes is endless. Just because a person is ‘smart’ doesn’t mean they have those characteristics. At the other extreme, I know a Down Syndrome girl who brightens my day every time I see her. She gives me a great big hug when we meet. She is always cheerful. I would destroy anyone who harmed her. Fortunately, she has people who love and care for her and she returns that love in so many ways. The area between mentally handicapped and brilliant is full of wonderful people who hold down jobs, love one another, raise beautiful children, do wonderful artwork and handicrafts, and in general live useful and fulfilled lives. Yes! It’s OK not to be ‘smart’.
